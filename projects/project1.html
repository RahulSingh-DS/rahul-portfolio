Problem Statement

The goal of this Kaggle competition was to predict whether a patient has heart disease based on 13 medical attributes including age, cholesterol level, blood pressure, ECG results, and maximum heart rate. The evaluation metric was ROC-AUC.

Dataset

The dataset contained approximately 630,000 records with 13 medical features:

AgeSexChest Pain TypeBlood Pressure CholesterolFasting Blood SugarECG Results Max Heart RateExercise AnginaST Depression Slope of STNo. of VesselsThallium Test

Target variable: Heart Disease (0 = Absence, 1 = Presence)

Approach & Methodology

01

Data Processing

Feature cleaning, target encoding, and dataset merging. Handled class imbalance carefully during preprocessing.

02

Cross Validation Strategy

Applied Stratified K-Fold (5 folds) to maintain class distribution across all validation splits and prevent data leakage.

03

Model Training

Trained LightGBM and XGBoost independently with hyperparameter tuning on each fold.

04

Ensemble

Final predictions generated by blending LightGBM and XGBoost outputs — improving generalization over single models.
